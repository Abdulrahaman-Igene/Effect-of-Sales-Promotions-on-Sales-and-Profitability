# Effect of Sales Promotions on Sales and Profitability

## Project Overview
This project analyzes the **effect of sales promotions on sales revenue and profitability** using a structured business dataset and tools such as **Microsoft Excel** and **Microsoft Power BI**.

The analysis evaluates how promotional activities influence sales performance, compares profit between promotional and non-promotional periods, and identifies the most profitable product categories and promotion types.

---

## Dataset Description
The dataset used in this project is **company_data 1.xlsx**. It contains transactional sales data across multiple regions, product categories, and promotion types.

### Key Columns in the Dataset
- **Order ID** – Unique identifier for each transaction  
- **Order Date** – Date of the sales transaction  
- **Unit Cost** – Cost per unit of the product  
- **Price** – Selling price per unit  
- **Order Qty** – Quantity ordered  
- **Cost of Sales** – Total cost incurred for the order  
- **Sales** – Total sales revenue  
- **Profit** – Profit generated from the transaction  
- **Channel** – Sales channel (Online or Store)  
- **Promotion Name** – Name of the promotional campaign  
- **Product Name** – Name of the product sold  
- **Manufacturer** – Product manufacturer  
- **Product Sub Category** – Sub-category of the product  
- **Product Category** – Main product category  
- **Region** – Sales region  
- **City** – City of sale  
- **Country** – Country of sale  

---

## Business Questions
The analysis addresses the following business questions:

1. What impact did sales promotions have on total sales revenue during the period covered by the data?
2. How did profit levels differ between periods with sales promotions and periods without sales promotions?
3. Which product or product category responded most positively to sales promotions in terms of profit?
4. Did sales promotions lead to higher sales but lower profit margins, or higher profit overall?
5. Which type of sales promotion generated the highest increase in sales volume?

---

## Key Observations
- Sales promotions accounted for approximately **67% of total sales**, indicating that promotional strategies were a major driver of customer purchases.
- Promotional sales generated about **66% of total profit**, showing that promotions were both high in volume and profitable.
- The **computer product category** recorded the highest profit among promoted products, suggesting stronger demand and/or higher margins.
- Overall, sales promotions resulted in a **significant increase in both sales and profit**, confirming their positive impact on business performance.
- The **American Back to School promotion** achieved the highest quantity ordered, highlighting the effectiveness of seasonal and targeted promotions.

---

## Summary of Findings
The analysis shows that sales promotions played a **critical role in improving business performance**. Promotional activities contributed the majority of total sales and profit, with the computer category emerging as the most profitable promotional segment. Seasonal campaigns such as the American Back to School promotion were especially effective in driving higher sales volumes. Overall, sales promotions proved to be a reliable strategy for increasing both revenue and profitability.
